1. Topic: Black Holes
Source: NASA's Black Hole Image news article (https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/edu/resources/teachable-moment/how-scientists-captured-the-first-image-of-a-black-hole/)
Summary: This article explains how researchers used a network of telescopes to take the first-ever picture of a black hole. It shows how much data processing is needed to turn telescope signals into a visual image.

2. Topic: Exoplanets
Source: Exoplanet Archive (NASA) (https://exoplanetarchive.ipac.caltech.edu/)
Summary: This is a huge database of every planet found outside our solar system. It lists things like their size, distance from their star, and how long their years are, which is good for sorting through with Python. 

3. Topic: Rocket Launches
Source: OpenRocket (Open-source simulator tool)
Summary: This is a tool that uses physics equations to predict how high a model rocket will go. It relates to my topic because I can use the same math to build a simple simulator in Python.
